Regional Vision Zero Traffic Safety Concerns

DVRPC and its partners are developing a Regional Vision Zero (RVZ) plan to make roadways safer for all users. As part of this process, we asked the public to share their safety concerns using this Traffic Safety Concerns Map. The comment period has closed and we will be sorting through the hundreds of comments recieved (thank you!). Comments gathered will help inform the RVZ plan by raising awareness of the public's traffic safety concerns.


Commenters were asked to select where the traffic safety concern occured, and then categorize based on the below nine categories, and enter additional details if desired.

  • Bicycle conditons
  • Near miss incident
  • Poor visibility
  • Road conditions
  • Sidewalk/crossing conditions/ADA facilities
  • Traffic signage/signals
  • Transit conditions
  • User behavior
  • Other

The map also displays DVRPC's Regional High Injury Network (RHIN) and the City of Philadelphia's Vision Zero High Injury Network layers: the red lines shows where two or more killed and/or severe injury (KSI) crashes occurred, and the blue lines shows where two or more crashes involving pedestrians or bicyclists occurred. The RHIN is a planning tool designed for use in tandem with other data sets to inform roadway safety investigations and for reference in plans and projects. Learn more about the Regional High Injury Network and the City of Philadelphia's Vision Zero High Injury Network.
